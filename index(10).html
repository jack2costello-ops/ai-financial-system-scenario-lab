Model architecture

The model runs annually from 2026 over a deliberately compressed horizon. AI adoption begins logistically, but a structural-regime index can activate when capability, autonomy, task substitution, machine contracting and legal recognition jointly cross a threshold. The phase regime changes elasticities rather than merely increasing the same linear effects: productivity can jump into double digits, labour income can decouple sharply from output, organisational networks can replace firm-based production, and funding can migrate at machine speed.

Core state transitions

AI adoption(t) = logistic(start, ceiling, adoption speed × transition compression) Structural signal = f(adoption, autonomy, effective automation, contracting, legal recognition, capability) Phase index = phase strength × sigmoid[(structural signal − threshold) / transition width] Productivity uplift = peak uplift × [incremental diffusion + nonlinear phase activation] Displaced stock(t) = prior stock + automation displacement + phase-break displacement − reallocation Labour share = start − automation rents − phase-regime rents − network rents + redistribution GDP growth = non-AI trend + productivity uplift − demand drag − financial drag − transition friction Deposit flight = routine switching + shock × agent-managed share × switching × model correlation CET1(t) = prior CET1 + retained earnings − credit/funding losses + policy response Systemic risk = weighted phase-transition + funding + credit + market-correlation + governance risks

Every coefficient is a modelling choice, not an estimated structural parameter. The controls expose the most consequential assumptions; further constants are documented in the JavaScript source.

How to use the lab

  1. Choose a preset that represents a coherent narrative.
  2. Change one cluster of assumptions at a time: capability, labour, finance or policy.
  3. Use the structural-break threshold and phase-strength controls to distinguish incremental diffusion from a genuine regime change.
  4. Look for threshold crossings and the speed of the transition rather than precise endpoint values.
  5. Run uncertainty analysis to see whether a conclusion survives modest parameter variation.
  6. Export paths and compare them with institution-level stress tests or macro models.

Interpretive cautions

  • The model does not price assets, solve a general equilibrium or model strategic agents explicitly.
  • “Logical coordination” is an experimental proxy. It does not assert that acausal trade is feasible.
  • Banking outputs are aggregate stylisations based on major-bank resilience ratios, not forecasts for individual firms.
  • Public data have different reference periods; each source card states its date.
