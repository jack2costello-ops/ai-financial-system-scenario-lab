Model architecture

The model runs annually from 2026. AI adoption follows a logistic curve. Autonomy, agent-managed finance and network production diffuse with adoption and legal/contracting conditions. Productivity raises supply; displacement, rent concentration and financial stress can reduce demand and weaken household balance sheets. Bank capital and liquidity respond to credit losses, margin pressure and deposit flight.

Core state transitions

AI adoption(t) = ceiling / [1 + ((ceiling/start) − 1)e^(−speed·t)] Effective automation = adoption × autonomy × task exposure × substitution × capability GDP growth = non-AI trend + productivity uplift − demand drag − financial drag − transition friction Displaced stock(t) = prior stock + new displacement − reallocation Labour share = start − capital rent capture + redistribution offset Deposit flight = routine switching + shock × agent-managed share × switching × model correlation CET1(t) = prior CET1 + retained earnings − credit losses − funding/transition costs + policy response Systemic risk = weighted funding + credit + market-correlation + operational + governance risks

Every coefficient is a modelling choice, not an estimated structural parameter. The controls expose the most consequential assumptions; further constants are documented in the JavaScript source.

How to use the lab

  1. Choose a preset that represents a coherent narrative.
  2. Change one cluster of assumptions at a time: capability, labour, finance or policy.
  3. Look for threshold crossings rather than precise endpoint values.
  4. Run uncertainty analysis to see whether a conclusion survives modest parameter variation.
  5. Export paths and compare them with institution-level stress tests or macro models.

Interpretive cautions

  • The model does not price assets, solve a general equilibrium or model strategic agents explicitly.
  • “Logical coordination” is an experimental proxy. It does not assert that acausal trade is feasible.
  • Banking outputs are aggregate stylisations based on major-bank resilience ratios, not forecasts for individual firms.
  • Public data have different reference periods; each source card states its date.
